Dual-Helmet Ignition Lock

The core novelty. The vehicle's ignition will not start unless both the rider's and pillion's helmets are securely worn, confirmed by strap sensors and RF communication.

Integrated Alcohol Detection

An MQ-3 sensor embedded in the rider's helmet analyzes breath for alcohol. If the level exceeds the legal limit, the ignition remains locked, preventing drunk driving.

üí•

Automatic Accident Detection

Using an MPU6050 accelerometer and gyroscope, the system detects sudden impacts or extreme tilts, automatically identifying a potential crash.

üî•

Smoke Detection

An MQ-2 sensor is included to detect smoke, which could indicate a fire after an accident, providing another layer of critical safety information.

IoT Emergency Notification

Upon accident detection, the onboard GSM and GPS modules send an automated SMS alert with precise coordinates to emergency contacts, ensuring rapid response.

Wireless System Integration

The helmets communicate wirelessly with the vehicle unit via RF transmitters and receivers, creating a seamless and reliable connection without cumbersome wires.

Applications & Societal Impact

This technology has far-reaching applications beyond individual safety, capable of influencing urban planning, law enforcement, and the insurance industry. Select a category to explore its potential.

Smart City Integration

Anonymized data on helmet compliance and accident locations can be fed into smart traffic systems. This helps city planners identify high-risk areas, optimize emergency service deployment, and create targeted road safety campaigns, making cities safer for everyone.

Incentives from Insurance

Insurance companies can offer lower premiums to riders using this system. Verifiable data on helmet use and safe driving habits (like no alcohol detection incidents) allows for more accurate risk assessment and can lead to a reduction in claims.

Effective Law Enforcement

This system acts as a proactive tool for enforcing traffic laws. It prevents violations before they happen (e.g., drunk driving, riding without a helmet) and provides crucial data for accident analysis, helping law enforcement agencies reduce fatalities.

Advanced Manufacturing

Motorcycle and helmet manufacturers can integrate this technology as a standard safety feature. Offering a vehicle with a built-in, advanced safety system provides a significant competitive advantage in a safety-conscious market.
